Child Safety Standards (CSAE/CSAM) — Heartpress

App: Heartpress
Developer: Technopresso (technopresso.com)
Effective date: 2026-01-28
Last updated: 2026-01-28

Heartpress has a zero-tolerance policy toward child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) and child sexual abuse material (CSAM). This document describes our published standards and enforcement related to CSAE/CSAM for Heartpress.

1) Eligibility and Age Requirement

Heartpress is intended for adults only.

  • Minimum age: 18+ (or the age of majority where the user resides, if higher).
  • Accounts that we determine (or reasonably suspect) belong to minors will be removed and may be permanently banned.
  • We may request additional verification steps when age is in question.

2) Prohibited Content and Behavior (Zero Tolerance)

Heartpress strictly prohibits any content, behavior, or activity that involves or promotes CSAE/CSAM, including (but not limited to):

  • Any sexual content involving a minor, or any sexualization of a minor
  • Any imagery, videos, or computer-generated content that depicts CSAM
  • Grooming, solicitation, coercion, “sextortion,” or attempts to obtain sexual content from minors
  • Human trafficking, facilitation, or exploitation involving minors
  • Sharing, requesting, distributing, or linking to CSAM
  • Attempts to evade detection or enforcement (e.g., coded language, redirecting to external platforms for CSAE)

Any CSAE/CSAM violation results in immediate enforcement, up to and including permanent removal and reporting as required.

3) In-App Reporting and User Feedback

Heartpress provides mechanisms for users to report concerns. Users can report:

  • Profiles
  • Messages / chats
  • Photos or other user-generated content
  • Any suspected CSAE/CSAM-related behavior

We encourage users to report immediately if they suspect a user is a minor or is engaging in CSAE-related activity.

4) How We Handle CSAE/CSAM Reports and “Actual Knowledge”

When we receive a report or otherwise obtain actual knowledge of potential CSAE/CSAM, we take appropriate action as promptly as practicable, which may include:

  • Reviewing the reported account/content
  • Removing content and/or disabling the account
  • Preserving relevant information where legally permitted/required for safety and compliance
  • Escalating to appropriate internal handling for child safety cases
  • Cooperating with lawful requests from law enforcement and relevant authorities

If a user believes a child is in immediate danger, they should contact local emergency services.

5) Reporting to Authorities (Where Required)

We maintain a process to report confirmed CSAM to the appropriate authority in the relevant jurisdiction, as required by law. This may include reporting to the relevant regional authority or designated reporting mechanisms (where applicable).

6) Enforcement Actions

Depending on severity and available evidence, enforcement actions may include:

  • Content removal
  • Account suspension
  • Permanent account termination (ban)
  • Device/account-level restrictions (where applicable)
  • Referral to authorities where required

There is no tolerance for CSAE/CSAM. Severe violations result in immediate removal.
